Derivatives & risk management

Derivatives are financial products whose value depends on the price of a specific asset.

Types of derivatives

There is a variety of derivatives: - forward contracts: agreement to purchase/sell at specific volume, date and price; - future contracts: standardised form of forward contracts, facilitated through an exchange (which handles any credit default risks) with a clearly defined price; - swaps: ... - European put/call: right (but no obligation) to buy/sell a specific volume at specific date and price; - American put/call: right (but no obligation) to buy/sell a specific volume at a specific price up to a specific date.
