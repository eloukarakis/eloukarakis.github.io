What's this about?

Excluding more complicated machine-learning algorithms, there's a whole bunch of more basic, statistical approaches to forecasting. Many of those involve using just the time-series itself rather than a large number of relevant features, and a relatively low computational cost.

(Moving) averages

The mean value of a time-series can often provide a very basic forecast, at least as long as the data is stationary and variance remains constant. The next best thing however when the time-series contains an underlying trend/seasonality can be a moving average: \[ f_{t+1}=1/k\cdot\Sigma_{t-k+1}^{t}y_t \] A moving average will always tend to lag the time-series.
While as a forecast this might not be necessarily be the best that can be, it can capture trends - useful for building decomposition models.
