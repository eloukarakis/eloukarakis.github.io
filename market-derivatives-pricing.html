Brownian Motion

Brownian motion is a random process \(\{X_t:t \ge 0\}\) with drift \(\mu\) and volatility \(\sigma\) if for \(s>0\): \[X_{t+s}-X_t \sim N(\mu \cdot s, \sigma^2 \cdot s)\] If drift is 0, and volatility is 1 then we have a standard Brownian motion.
Geometric Brownian motion is a random process if for \(t>0\) and \(W_t\) a standard Brownian motion: \( X_t=e^{(\mu-\sigma^2/2) \cdot t + \sigma \cdot W_t} \). Consequently: \[ X_{t+s} = X_t \cdot e^{(\mu-\sigma^2/2) \cdot s + \sigma \cdot (W_{t+s} - W_t)} \]
